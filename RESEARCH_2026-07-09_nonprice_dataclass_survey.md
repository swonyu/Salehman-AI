## Family 1 — Profitability / quality long leg (GP/A, QMJ): SURVIVES ON PAPER, ≈NULL AT RETAIL TODAY
- **CONFIRMED:** Gross profitability (Novy-Marx 2013, JFE 108(1):1–28, primary PDF read): long-quintile FF3 alpha +0.34%/mo (t=5.01); annual rebalance ⇒ positions turn over ~once per 4 years ⇒ ~3bps/yr drag at the engine's 13bps RT — **the one family where retail costs do not bind** (Novy-Marx & Velikov 2016 confirms net survival + capacity).
- **REVISED (verifiers confirmed the stats verbatim, tightened the framing):** Blitz-Baltussen-van Vliet (FAJ 2020, open-access PDF): profitability's SHORT leg has the higher standalone Sharpe, yet the long leg retains significant alpha over short-spanned benchmarks (+0.51%/yr t=2.21; shorts spanned p=0.87) — long-only implementation is defensible in principle.
- **CONFIRMED (the decision-relevant honesty-floor finding, independently re-derived by all 3 verifiers from re-downloaded primary data):** current era is already measured ≈null — AQR's own author-maintained QMJ monthly dataset (aqr.com, fetched this session): USA QMJ post-2013 +2.62%/yr, **t=1.01** (~37% decay, inside the McLean-Pontiff band, insignificant); the live long-only retail implementation (QUAL ETF vs SPY, Yahoo v8 adjclose fetched this session) **lost to SPY by 0.6–1.4%/yr net over 2013–2026**. Computed stats: [research/quality_family_computed_2026-07-09.csv](research/quality_family_computed_2026-07-09.csv).
- **Disposition:** do NOT promote standalone. At most a cheap GP/A-as-secondary-sort incremental ablation (long-leg-only, real Yahoo fundamentals, DSR-gated) QUEUED BEHIND higher-EV work. Note: Yahoo quoteSummary fundamentals modules were NOT live-verified this session (crumb caveat) — verify before any implementation. Accruals (Sloan 1996) deliberately excluded: short-leg-concentrated, long-dead post-publication — omission is a decision, not an oversight.

## Family 3 — Monthly-horizon PEAD (SUE, 30–60d holds): NULL-LEANING REFUSE (documented absence)
- **REVISED (core confirmed; peripheral numeric glosses tightened):** the historical effect is real — Bernard & Thomas (JAR 1989): ~+2% abnormal over 60 trading days, monotone across SUE deciles; at monthly holds turnover falls to ~4–8 RT/name-year, so the 1–5d cost objection (the corpus's existing PEAD null) genuinely does not apply — this family was distinct, not a rerun.
- **REVISED/CONFIRMED-core (the pincer):** the binding constraint MOVED rather than vanished. - **Disposition:** REFUSE as a candidate. Data path is fully UNBLOCKED (earnings dates via the proven cookie+crumb fetcher; the persisted 61-name panel) so a confirmatory in-app SUE-sort ablation is cheap if the corpus ever wants an owned measurement of the null — low priority, prior firmly null.
