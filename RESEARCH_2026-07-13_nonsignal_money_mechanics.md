## The question

The campaign EXHAUSTIVELY proved no predictive SIGNAL/alpha edge survives net-of-cost at retail
(every data class surveyed/ablated = null/refuse). This is the DISTINCT, genuinely-uncovered question:
which NON-SIGNAL, MECHANICAL money-levers improve a retail after-tax/after-fee/after-behavior P&L
WITHOUT needing to predict returns? (0 corpus hits on tax/harvest/wash-sale/behavioral-gap before this
run.) The owner's north star is "the fastest way to make money" — and when no alpha exists, THIS is the
honest answer space.

## Per-lever disposition (sourced)

- **Fund/advisory-fee minimization — REAL, best-quantified.** ~50–100 bps/yr; a 1%/yr fee compounds to
  ~33% of a 40-yr terminal balance (0.99⁴⁰=0.669). This is **Sharpe's arithmetic (1991) — a
  mathematical IDENTITY, not a forecast**: after costs the average active dollar must lag the average
  passive dollar by exactly the fee gap. Active US funds ~0.62% vs passive ~0.12% (Morningstar/ICI
  2020). The single most certain lever in the survey; caps at the fee you already pay (an index
  investor has ~nothing left to harvest).
- **Tax-loss harvesting — REAL-BUT-SMALL, half-marketing.** Chaudhuri-Burnham-Lo (FAJ 76(3):99-108,
  2020, CRSP top-500 1926-2018): 1.08%/yr GROSS tax alpha → **0.82%** after the wash-sale rule (IRC
  §1091) → ~0.94-0.95% after transaction cost; the four 23-yr sub-periods decline 2.13→0.51→1.08→**0.81%**
  (recent-window ~81 bps, NOT the marketed ~1.5%). Two gutting catches: (1) it is **DEFERRAL not
  elimination** — harvesting lowers cost basis, so gains are recognized later unless step-up-at-death /
  donation (which the app can't model); (2) ~0 for a low-bracket or tax-advantaged (IRA/401k) account;
  front-loaded (~80% of savings in first 5 years, Wealthfront/JPM) and decaying as basis resets.
- **Broker-choice execution — REAL, one-time.** Schwarz-Barber-Huang-Jorion-Odean (J. Finance 2025),
  85,000 simultaneous market orders: mean account-level round-trip cost ranged **0.07%-0.46% across 6
  brokers for the SAME order** (wholesalers price-improve different brokers differently). A one-time
  controllable dispersion (pick the right broker), not a compounding annual yield.
- **Margin-rate arbitrage — cost-reduction on debt only.** IBKR 5.14-6.14% vs Schwab/Fidelity 11.825%
  sub-$25k (corpus fastest-money, 2026-07-09) — ~5.7pp/yr on BORROWED dollars, and the leverage survey
  already ruled margin CLOSED-NEGATIVE (1x diversified beta is the prudent max this era). Not a return.
- **Securities lending — marginal.** ~few bps to ~0.2%/yr for liquid holders on a 50/50 retail split
  (IBKR SYEP, Schwab FPSL); general-collateral rebate ≈0, only hard-to-borrow "specials" pay.

## The two myths (the valuable debunks — now sourced in-corpus)

- **Behavioral "return gap" (fund-timing) — MYTH-TIER, ~3-10 bps.** Fulkerson-Jordan-Riley-Yan (FAJ,
  Apr 2026) correct THREE math errors in Morningstar's own data: the "Mind the Gap" 1.7%/yr collapses
  to **0.03%**, the 1.2% version to 0.10%. DALBAR's ~4-6%/yr is long-discredited. So "avoid mistiming
  funds" is worth ~3-10 bps — effectively zero. The ONLY genuinely large behavioral number is
  Barber-Odean's ~1.5%/yr (average household) to ~7pp (highest-vs-lowest turnover quintile) — but that
  is ENTIRELY transaction costs/turnover, NOT timing, and is the ALREADY-SHIPPED lever (RefuseList +
  net-cost floor). Do NOT build a fund-timing warning for a ≈0 effect.
- **Rebalancing bonus / vol-harvesting — MYTH-TIER, ~0 excess.** For a CORRELATED retail equity/60-40
  book the "diversification return" (Willenbrock 2011) is ~0-1.1%/yr GROSS but is NOT excess over a
  fixed-proportion benchmark, is offset by the winner-give-up (Chambers-Zdanowicz 2014), and is erased
  by costs + trending markets → defensible net excess ≈ 0 bps/yr. The 4.3-4.5%/yr headlines require
  UNCORRELATED ~30%-vol zero-drift assets (commodity futures 4.52%; 40 uncorrelated 30%-vol names 4.3%)
  a retail equity book does not have. Corpus already found sub-monthly rebalancing is pure cost.
- **Sequence-of-returns as a return lever — identity ZERO.** Terminal wealth of a no-flow book =
  Π(1+r_t), commutative — order is mathematically irrelevant without cashflows. Real only in
  decumulation/contribution phases as a risk-of-ruin issue, NOT a return lever.

## The practical synthesis — the honest "fastest way to make money"

Given no alpha exists, the fastest-money answer the mechanics support is **the boring one, quantified**:
on a diversified beta exposure, **minimize fees + execution cost + tax drag, and stop overtrading.**
Stackable defensible after-friction budget for a high-bracket US taxable investor ≈ **~1.0-1.9%/yr
recurring** (fund fees ~50-100 + TLH ~40-90, high-bracket taxable only) **plus a one-time ~39 bps
round-trip** from broker choice. Real money that compounds — but a **cost/tax floor-raise, not an edge**,
an order of magnitude below what alpha would have needed (and alpha is proven absent). The two myths
contribute ≈0 and should not be sold as levers.

## What this survey did NOT establish

- **Not an alpha edge, not a signal.** Every real lever is a cost/tax friction reduction on beta — it
  raises the after-friction floor, does not beat the market. The DSR≈0 / no-proven-edge floor is untouched.
- **No empirical ablation** — survey-grade synthesis of 3-vote-verified literature (academic point
  estimates), not a net-of-cost walk-forward; the TLH/broker numbers are not measured on the owner's fills.
- **TLH is not universal** — high-bracket / US / taxable-account-specific / front-loaded / decaying /
  DEFERRAL; ~0 for low-bracket or tax-advantaged; permanence needs step-up/donation the app can't model.
- **Broker-execution 39 bps is a one-time round-trip dispersion**, not a compounding annual yield;
  US-market, market-order, 2015-era-sample-anchored.
- **No account-level data path exists in the app** (no tax status, no cost basis, no account-broker
  field) — so every feature candidate is data-blocked, not build-ready.

**Net:** the non-signal money-mechanics space is surveyed. Two real levers (fee-min, TLH), both
cost/tax floor-raises not alpha, both order-of-magnitude below any signal edge; three myths debunked
with primary sources. Milestone (net-of-cost DSR>0.95 ALPHA edge) UNMET and unchanged — this space was
never going to contain alpha, only friction reduction. Nothing wired; feature candidates
account-data-blocked + owner-gated.
