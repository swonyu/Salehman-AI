**Date:** 2026-07-12 · **Disposition: REFUSE-and-index. 0 survivors. The accessible non-price

## The question

Every prior price-side null in the corpus cited an "unsurveyed non-price escape hatch." Ad-hoc
surveys had closed exactly two (news-sentiment, short-interest). This run **systematically
enumerates** the untested-but-accessible space: the 8 data classes with ZERO corpus coverage that
are fetchable at the owner's tier ($29.99 EODHD All World + connected MCPs: alphavantage,
coingecko, EODHD, Stocks Intelligence). Each judged against the three-part bar:
1. **INCREMENTAL-TO-PRICE** — adds power over price/size/value/momentum + lagged returns; not
   spanned by families already null on the 34,701-series survivorship-free panel.
2. **SURVIVORSHIP-FREE COVERAGE** — the signal exists for delisted names (or is reconstructable);
   else any ablation is survivorship-lite untestably.
3. **RETAIL-COST-SURVIVABLE** — survives the ratified costs (US 13 / intl 30 / EM 60 / .SR 60 /
   crypto 70 bps RT) AND is not concentrated in a leg retail can't occupy (short / high-borrow /
   microcap).
Haircut every published effect 26% OOS / 58% post-pub (McLean-Pontiff; US = most-reliable decay,
Jacobs-Müller) BEFORE judging.

## HEADLINE — the space is exhausted, 0 survivors

All 8 classes fail the bar on at least one **structural** axis (not a marginal number). The
failures cluster into three walls; every class hits ≥1:

- **Retail-cost wall** — gross edge → ≈0 net at the ratified tier (options-IV, analyst-revisions,
  transcripts, dividend-drift; options via the option itself ≈60-100× the equity tier).
- **Short-leg wall** — the tradeable weight is a short leg retail cannot occupy (13F
  change-in-breadth, analyst-recs, dividend-omission drift are long-short books dominated by the
  un-shortable leg).
- **Survivorship wall** — the vendor feed is survivors-only / has no delisted coverage
  (options-underlyings = current optionable universe; 13F = current snapshot; analyst consensus
  ceases at delisting; transcript floor 2010Q1 excludes the 2008 lane).

This closes the "unsurveyed non-price escape hatches" residual that every prior price-side null
cited — now proven for the higher-signal non-price families the corpus had left open. **The escape
hatches are measured and empty.** Milestone (net-of-cost DSR>0.95) remains UNMET.

## Closest-to-alive — dividend-change / distribution drift

Ranked closest for a specific reason: it is the ONLY class where **survivorship is
RECONSTRUCTABLE at the owner tier** — ex-div dates/amounts are persistent corporate-action events
on the survivorship-free panel (`get_historical_dividends`), the exact property that killed
news-sentiment's D1. It clears the survivorship wall the other seven fail. It STILL dies because
it hits the other two walls simultaneously: the economically-dominant tradeable weight is the
dividend-omission SHORT leg (un-occupiable), and the modern long-side drift is priced in ~1 day
(the Martineau/PEAD collapse) → ≈0 net current-era. Reconstructable data does not rescue a signal
whose alpha is in the short leg and whose long leg has decayed to announcement-day.

## What this survey did NOT establish (mandatory)

- **Survey, not ablation.** No class ran through the net-of-cost DSR gate. A REFUSE here =
  "not worth a prereg given the wall," NOT "empirically disproven on the frozen panel." The
  closest-to-alive dividend class would still need a full prereg+DSR run to be a *measured* null —
  it is refused pre-ablation on cost+short-leg grounds (permitted for structurally-walled classes,
  but weaker than a measured null).
- **Access assumptions are point-in-time; the EODHD MCP token was EXPIRED at probe time.** The
  credit "no per-name endpoint" and holdings "snapshot-only" findings rest on tool SCHEMAS (still
  decisive) not live pulls; the dividend + transcript access claims should be re-confirmed live
  before any prereg. A schema-based access refusal is weaker than a measured 401/empty.
- **Scope is US + owner-tier.** OptionMetrics IvyDB (survivorship-complete options), CRSP-grade
  13F as-of panels, and institutional single-name CDS are out of scope — several classes are
  refused as INACCESSIBLE, not DISPROVEN. A future owner data-purchase changes the access verdict,
  not necessarily the edge verdict.
- **The 8 are not the whole non-price universe.** NOT surveyed: alternative/web data (satellite,
  card-spend, web-traffic, app-downloads), supply-chain/relationship graphs, short-interest
  *dynamics*, patent/R&D text, per-name hiring/JOLTS, ESG-change, and options *order-flow/GEX/DEX*
  (distinct from IV-surface). "Exhausted" applies to *these 8 accessible classes* + the corpus's
  statement that no owner-tier surveyed class survives — it is a scoping boundary, not universal
  coverage.
- **Incremental-to-price assessed by argument, not regression.** The PARTIAL labels (options-IV,
  13F, analyst-revisions) mean "plausibly distinct" — none horse-raced against the shipped price
  features. A PARTIAL that cleared cost+survivorship would still owe a distinctness ablation.
