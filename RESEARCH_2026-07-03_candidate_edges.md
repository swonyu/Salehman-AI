## Validation-candidates (backlog — queued for `tools/altdata_ablation` once a split-adjusted equity feed exists)

### 1. Turn-of-month / intramonth seasonality (Ariel 1987; McConnell-Xu 2008) — long-only index-ETF (SPY), in near month-end, out ~day+3

- **Paper effect (GROSS):** Definition: 4-day TOM window = last trading day of month through 3rd trading day of next month. Ariel (1987, JFE), US 1963-1981: positive mean returns concentrated in the first half of the month, ~0 in the second half. Lakonishok-Smidt (1988), DJIA 1897-1986: cumulative return over the 4 TOM days = 0.473% vs 0.349% for the whole month (rest-of-month net negative). McConnell-Xu (2008, FAJ), the strongest anchor — CRSP value-weighted, OUT-OF-SAMPLE 1987-2005: +0.14%/day excess over the 4 TOM days vs -0.01%/day over the other 16 days => ~0.56%/mo gross concentrated in the window (~6.7%/yr, arithmetic). Equal-weighted OOS: 0.24%/day vs 0.04%/day. Effect present in 31 of 35 countries. All figures GROSS; the concentrated window essentially captures the entire equity risk premium, not an alpha over buy-and-hold.
- **After haircut:** Base = 0.56%/mo gross (McConnell-Xu VW, OOS 1987-2005 window sum). McConnell-Xu is ALREADY a ~19-yr out-of-sample re-test of Lakonishok-Smidt, so the mechanical 26% OOS haircut is partly pre-paid; I apply the harsher 58% post-publication haircut as the binding case (the effect has been public and tradable for 15+ years since 2008). 58% haircut => ~0.24%/mo (~2.8%/yr). Using the milder 26% OOS haircut => ~0.41%/mo (~5.0%/yr). I anchor on the 58% figure because recent evidence (below) shows real post-2005 fade beyond the statistical haircut.
- **Net-of-cost verdict:** Natural implementation: long-only INDEX-ETF (SPY), one round trip per month (in near month-end, out on day+3). Turnover ~1 round trip/month = 12/yr. StockSage index-ETF round-trip cost = 8bps. Net after 58% haircut: 0.235%/mo - 0.08% = ~0.155%/mo => ~1.9%/yr NET. Per-day cost drag (8bps/4 days = 2bps/day) < post-pub daily edge (~5.9bps/day), roughly 3:1 cushion. So on the arithmetic it SURVIVES retail costs at the ETF tier — a genuinely rare result. Break-even framing: this is a directional timing trade, not a hit-rate bet, so p*=1/(1+netRR) is less apt; the binding comparison is net window return (~1.9%/yr) vs the opportunity cost of NOT holding the index the other 16 days (buy-and-hold ~6-7%/yr gross). Net-of-cost the strategy underperforms buy-and-hold on total return; its only genuine edge is Sharpe/exposure-reduction (Quantpedia cites ~7.2%/yr, Sharpe 1.04 for ~4 days/mo of market exposure), NOT excess total return.
- **Why ranked #1:** Ranked #1 on net-cost-feasibility-per-risk. It is the single cleanest arithmetic survivor: cheapest cost tier in the whole set (index-ETF 8bps round-trip, not the 13bps large-cap or 100-300bps microcap tiers the dead candidates need), lowest turnover (~12 round-trips/yr, far under any fence), and a ~3:1 cushion of post-pub daily edge (~5.9bps/day) over per-day cost drag (~2bps/day) → ~1.9%/yr NET. Zero microcap dependence, holds value-weighted in large/high-price stocks (McConnell-Xu), so it does not touch fence #6. HONESTY FLOOR CAVEAT that caps this at a candidate, not a win: net-of-cost it UNDERPERFORMS buy-and-hold on TOTAL return — the genuine edge is Sharpe / market-exposure-reduction (Quantpedia ~7.2%/yr, Sharpe ~1.04 for ~4 days/mo of exposure), NOT excess total return. Ship framing must be exposure-efficiency, never 'beats the index'. It is also a pure calendar-timing overlay orthogonal to every shipped signal (vol-regime, vol-targeting, TSMOM 12-1, RS, 52wk-high, left-tail/vol-of-vol), so it is a new axis, not a repackage.
- **Ablation recipe:** tools/altdata_ablation, single-symbol SPY (plus 1-2 large-cap index ETFs as robustness), ~5y daily adjusted, walk-forward (signal at bar i uses only data <=i, fill at i+1 open). Define the window by trading-day index: enter at the close of the last trading day BEFORE month-end offset t=-1, exit at the close of trading day t=+3 (McConnell-Xu window; sweep t in {-1..-2} entry and {+3..+4} exit as robustness, do NOT cherry-pick the best). Costs = StockSageNetEdge.defaultCosts for the ETF tier (~8bps round-trip) applied once per monthly round-trip. PRIMARY METRIC IS NOT total return — it is risk-adjusted: report (a) net annualized window return, (b) net Sharpe of the in-market days vs buy-and-hold Sharpe, (c) net return-per-unit-market-exposure (window days are ~4/mo vs ~21). Non-overlapping monthly blocks, block-level significance on the window-return series; Deflated Sharpe via StockSageDeflatedSharpe against estimatedStrategyTrials that COUNTS the entry/exit-offset sweep as trials. PASS only if net window Sharpe clears buy-and-hold Sharpe AND DSR>0.95; a positive window return that loses to buy-and-hold on total return is an exposure-efficiency result, index it as such (roadmap #4/frontier), do NOT wire as an alpha ranking change.
- **Did NOT establish:** DID NOT ESTABLISH: (1) that the effect still nets positive in the MOST RECENT decade — multiple secondary sources report the TOM effect has 'largely disappeared over the last ~20 years' / faded post-2005; I could not obtain a clean, primary, dated post-2010 US magnitude (the Arkansas 'Relook' paper 403'd), so the ~1.9%/yr net figure rests on the 1987-2005 OOS window carried through the 58% haircut, NOT on fresh post-2010 data. This is the single biggest open risk and the walk-forward must confirm it on recent bars. (2) I did NOT establish that it beats buy-and-hold on TOTAL return net of cost — it does not; its case is Sharpe/exposure-efficiency only, which is a different objective than the engine's edge goal. (3) I did NOT verify the exact FAJ-published (vs working-draft) point estimates or the significance t-stats first-hand. (4) Cost figure assumes retail ETF execution near the close; slippage on the last-day-of-month close (a high-volume, sometimes gap-prone print) could exceed 8bps and was not modeled. Honesty floor: every magnitude above is labeled gross vs net and sourced+dated; recent-decade persistence is a documented ABSENCE, not a confirmation.
- **Sources:** Ariel, R.A. (1987). A monthly effect in stock returns. Journal of Financial Economics 18(1): 161-174.; Lakonishok, J. & Smidt, S. (1988). Are seasonal anomalies real? A ninety-year perspective. Review of Financial Studies 1(4): 403-425 (as cited in McConnell-Xu for the 4-day DJIA figures).; McConnell, J.J. & Xu, W. (2008). Equity Returns at the Turn of the Month. Financial Analysts Journal 64(2): 49-64 (working-paper draft July 2006 read directly for the 1987-2005 OOS daily figures).; McLean, R.D. & Pontiff, J. (2016). Does Academic Research Destroy Stock Return Predictability? Journal of Finance 71(1): 5-32 (26% OOS / 58% post-publication decay).; Kunkel, Compton & Beyer (2003), 'The turn-of-the-month effect still lives: the international evidence,' International Review of Financial Analysis (15/19 countries, TOM ~87% of monthly return, 1988-2000).
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM · **3-vote:** 0/3 refuted

### 2. Residual / idiosyncratic momentum (Blitz-Huij-Martens 2011; Gutierrez-Prinsky) — long-only tilt on FF3-residualized 12-1 return rank, liquid large-caps

- **Paper effect (GROSS):** GROSS, long-short WML deciles, monthly rebalanced, US CRSP. Blitz-Hanauer-Vidojevic: idiosyncratic momentum ~1.39%/mo gross avg return, monthly Sharpe 0.48 (US Dec-1925–Dec-2015) vs conventional momentum 1.54%/mo, Sharpe 0.25 — i.e. ~90% comparable return at roughly HALF the volatility, so Sharpe ~doubles. Blitz-Huij-Martens (2011, JEF 18(3):506-521): residual momentum ~doubles the risk-adjusted profit of total-return momentum and cuts crash risk (US 1926-2009). Quantpedia's replicated LONG-ONLY-style 12-1 residual factor: 9.18%/yr, vol 15.27%, Sharpe 0.34, MaxDD -59.74% (1926-2009) — note the long-only variant Sharpe is far below the 0.48 long-short figure, and the drawdown is still large. Gutierrez-Pirinsky (2007, J.Fin.Markets): the residual/abnormal-return leg does NOT reverse in yr2-5 (+0.20%/mo months 13-60) whereas relative-return momentum reverses hard (-0.40%/mo) — evidence the residual signal is underreaction, not a compensated-risk or reversal artifact.
- **After haircut:** Effect is published (2011 primary; 2020 anomaly paper) → the ~58% POST-PUBLICATION McLean-Pontiff haircut applies, not the 26% OOS. On Sharpe: 0.48 gross long-short → ~0.20 haircut-adjusted; the long-only replicated 0.34 → ~0.14. On return: the relevant retail figure is the LONG leg of a long-only tilt, ~0.4-0.6%/mo gross above market (long leg carries the smaller half of the long-short spread) → after 58% haircut ~0.17-0.25%/mo gross. I used the post-publication (larger) haircut deliberately — this factor has been public and traded by Robeco/AQR-style shops for a decade, so decay is real and already partly realized.
- **Net-of-cost verdict:** Turnover: conventional momentum top-decile ~28% one-sided/mo (Novy-Marx-Velikov); idiosyncratic momentum is 'modestly higher' (author's own words) — call it ~32% one-sided/mo → ~64% two-sided/mo → ~770% annualized two-sided. At US large-cap RETAIL round-trip 13bps (StockSageNetEdge): 7.7 turns/yr × 13bps ≈ 100bps/yr ≈ 0.083%/mo cost drag on the long leg. Net long-only: ~0.17-0.25%/mo gross(post-haircut) − 0.083%/mo cost ≈ +0.09-0.17%/mo NET at retail. This is one of the few anomalies where break-even cost sits ABOVE retail large-cap cost: Blitz-Hanauer-Vidojevic report iMOM's break-even transaction cost is ~15% HIGHER than conventional momentum, and momentum's one-sided monthly turnover (~28-32%) is below the ~50% Novy-Marx-Velikov net-survival line. Break-even win rate is not the natural frame for a continuous factor tilt, but as a sanity check p*=1/(1+netRR): at a ~1:1 net reward-risk the tilt needs >50% hit-rate, and momentum's monthly hit-rate on the long leg is ~53-55% historically — thin but positive. CAVEAT that keeps confidence at MEDIUM: retail's SMALL name universe forces coarse quantiles (not clean deciles) and pushes effective turnover and per-name cost up vs the institutional figure, and the long-only long leg is where momentum is weakest — the net could compress to ~0. Net is plausibly positive but SMALL; it does not blow up negative the way naive reversal does (that was -1.28%/mo net, t=-6.02, in the week-horizon run).
- **Why ranked #2:** Ranked #2. It is the only candidate here with a genuinely positive net TOTAL-return edge at retail large-cap (~+0.09-0.17%/mo net after ~0.083%/mo cost drag at 13bps and ~64% two-sided monthly turnover, which sits UNDER the Novy-Marx-Velikov ~50%-one-sided net-survival line and inside the fence). Break-even transaction cost is ~15% ABOVE conventional momentum (Blitz-Hanauer-Vidojevic) and the paper effect is explicitly haircut in this verdict, not taken at face value (clears fence #7). Distinct from shipped TSMOM 12-1 (that is total-return time-series own-trend; this is cross-sectional rank of factor-hedged residuals) with low expected correlation and spanning-regression support (iMOM alpha survives with TSMOM controlled; converse does not hold). Ranked BELOW turn-of-month despite higher total-return upside because it carries MORE risk-per-unit-feasibility: retail's small liquid universe forces coarse quantiles (not clean deciles), pushing effective turnover and per-name cost above the institutional figure, and the long-only long leg is momentum's WEAKEST leg — the honest verdict says net 'plausibly positive but SMALL' and could compress toward ~0. It does not blow up negative (unlike naive reversal at -1.28%/mo net), so it is worth measuring, but it is the more fragile of the two survivors.
- **Ablation recipe:** tools/altdata_ablation, cross-sectional long-only over the liquid large-cap universe, ~5y+ daily/monthly. Each month: (1) regress each name's trailing returns on FF3 (mkt/size/value) over the formation window, (2) form the 12-1 CUMULATIVE RESIDUAL momentum (skip the most recent month), (3) rank cross-sectionally, take the top long-only quantile (use COARSE quantiles matching the real small universe — quintiles/terciles, NOT deciles — so turnover and per-name cost are realistic, not institutional-idealized). Walk-forward: signal at month t uses only data <=t, fill at t+1 open. Costs = StockSageNetEdge.defaultCosts US large-cap (~13bps round-trip) applied to measured two-sided turnover (do NOT assume the ~64% figure — MEASURE realized turnover on the coarse-quantile portfolio). Non-overlapping monthly blocks, block-level significance; DSR via StockSageDeflatedSharpe with estimatedStrategyTrials counting the formation-window and quantile-width sweeps as trials. Report NET (not gross) monthly alpha with CI, realized two-sided monthly turnover, and a spanning regression of this signal's returns on the shipped TSMOM 12-1 return series (PASS on distinctness requires residual alpha to survive with TSMOM controlled). PASS only if net monthly alpha CI excludes 0 AND DSR>0.95; if net compresses to ~0 (the flagged risk), index the null and close — do NOT wire a coin-flip tilt.
- **Did NOT establish:** I did NOT establish a NET, LONG-ONLY, RETAIL-SCALE, SMALL-UNIVERSE figure for this engine — every headline number in the literature is GROSS, long-short, decile-based, institutional, and monthly-shorting. My net estimate (~+0.09-0.17%/mo) is a derived projection built on assumed turnover (~32% one-sided, interpolated from 'modestly higher than conventional's 28%', not a directly reported iMOM number) and an assumed long-leg share of the spread (~half) — both are estimates, labeled as such, and the true retail net could compress toward zero once the small-universe coarse-quantile penalty and long-leg weakness bite. I did NOT run the ablation; the empirical validation gate (walk-forward + deflated Sharpe + net-of-cost, per research-memory §2) is UNMET. I could NOT fetch the ScienceDirect / SSRN primary PDFs (HTTP 403) — the Blitz-Hanauer-Vidojevic and Huij-Lansdorp 'Revisited' figures come from Alpha Architect/CXO/Quantpedia secondary summaries and the SSRN abstract, so the exact break-even-cost basis-point number and the precise iMOM turnover % are second-hand and should be confirmed against the paper before promotion. I did NOT verify the long-only variant's Sharpe beyond Quantpedia's single 0.34 replication (which is notably below the 0.48 long-short figure and carries a -59.7% max drawdown — a real crash-risk caveat that partly undercuts the 'lower crash risk' selling point in a long-only wrapper). ## Anti-edges (documented — die net-of-cost at retail; do NOT build)

- Vol-managed / vol-timing of the momentum factor (Moreira-Muir; Barroso-Santa-Clara) — DIES on the SUBSTRATE, not the overlay. 3-vote refuted 2/3 (survives:false). The timing overlay is cheap (a few bps/mo, clears its own break-even), but the effect it overlays is the WML LONG-SHORT factor, which retail cannot hold: it needs a short leg and up to ~864% leverage at the 99th pct of the scaled position (Cederburg). Retail's tradable long-only, leverage-capped momentum is precisely the constrained form where most of the paper gain evaporates. The overlay survives costs; the factor it overlays does not exist in retail-tradable form at the paper's magnitude. KILL.
- Betting-against-beta / low-volatility anomaly (Frazzini-Pedersen; Baker-Bradley-Wurgler) — DIES NET AT RETAIL despite surviving the distinctness vote (survives:true, 0 refuted — the vote only certified it is not already-shipped and not a fenced label, NOT that it makes money). Novy-Marx-Velikov show the alpha is manufactured by micro-cap equal-weighting: realistic BAB trading cost is 60bps/mo standard, 23bps/mo even after aggressive large-cap mitigation, vs a post-haircut gross of only ~16-23bps/mo → net NEGATIVE or economically trivial (+11bps/mo only in the single best-case cost-mitigated large-cap decade, inside noise). The mechanism's core lever (lever up low-beta) is also unavailable to this engine. Falls to fenced anti-edge #6 (edge lives in the bottom 1% of market cap where retail spreads devour it). Distinct label, identical fate. KILL.
- MAX / lottery-demand effect (Bali-Cakici-Whitelaw) — DIES NET AT RETAIL despite surviving the distinctness vote (survives:true, 1 refuted). The profitable leg is the SHORT leg in median-$6.47 / median-$21.5M microcaps — realistic all-in round-trip there is 100-300+bps, not the 13bps large-cap assumption — so the ~1%/mo gross × ~7 round-trips/yr is fully consumed (fence #6). As a long-only large-cap avoid-screen the cost is ~zero but so is the residual edge (large-cap high-MAX names carry little of the anomaly). Also a fence #7 face-value-effect-size trap (the -1.18%/mo is a gross VW long-short the engine can't hold), and it economically overlaps the engine's existing left-tail / high-IVOL-avoidance instincts (MAX largely subsumes the IVOL puzzle). Net ≈ 0 or negative either way. KILL.
